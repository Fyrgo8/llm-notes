## Abstract

Recently, large language models have shown strong reasoning ability under large-scale reinforcement learning, but effective multi-tool collaborative reasoning remains difficult. Tool-Star proposes a reinforcement-learning-based framework for stepwise multi-tool reasoning. The method combines a scalable tool-use data synthesis pipeline, a two-stage training setup with cold-start supervised fine-tuning and self-critic reinforcement learning, and a hierarchical reward that explicitly rewards correct and well-formatted answers while encouraging real multi-tool collaboration. The paper reports gains on more than ten computational and knowledge-intensive reasoning benchmarks.
